Intellectual Property Rights

This document describes ownership, licences and reuse of intellectual property created during projects.

  1. Ownership of existing IP

    smartflux sites retains all rights to its existing intellectual property, including but not limited to: proprietary design systems, reusable code components, UI patterns and technical methods developed prior to or independently of the agreement.

  2. Delivery and ownership transfer

    Upon full payment, smartflux sites transfers the rights to the design and website code specifically developed for the Client to the Client. The Client is free to use, modify and have it managed.

    Reusable components and general code blocks over which smartflux sites previously held rights remain the property of smartflux sites — the Client receives a perpetual licence to use these.

  3. Client materials

    The Client remains the sole owner of all provided content, images, logos, texts and other business materials. The Client warrants that provided materials are royalty-free or that the Client has the right to use them. smartflux sites is not liable for infringement of third-party rights resulting from materials provided by the Client.

  4. The "building blocks" clause

    The Client acknowledges that smartflux sites serves multiple clients. smartflux sites retains the right to reuse general code components, layout structures and technical solutions developed during the project for other Clients, provided no Client-specific confidential information, brand identity or personal data is included.

  5. Full ownership transfer (buy-out option)

    As standard, smartflux sites delivers websites where reusable components are licensed as described in Section 2. If the Client wishes full ownership of all underlying code and components without reservation, this must be explicitly agreed in the quote. An additional "IP buy-out" fee applies.

  6. Use as reference

    smartflux sites retains the right to mention delivered websites as a reference and display them in its own portfolio, unless the Client has indicated in writing that they object to this.
